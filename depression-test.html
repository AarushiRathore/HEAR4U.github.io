Depression Self-Assessment

This questionnaire is based on common depression screening tools. Please answer honestly about how you've been feeling over the past two weeks. Remember, this is not a diagnosis but can help identify symptoms you may want to discuss with a healthcare provider.

1. Over the last two weeks, how often have you felt little interest or pleasure in doing things?

2. Over the last two weeks, how often have you felt down, depressed, or hopeless?

3. Over the last two weeks, how often have you had trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much?

4. Over the last two weeks, how often have you felt tired or had little energy?

5. Over the last two weeks, how often have you had poor appetite or overeaten?

6. Over the last two weeks, how often have you felt bad about yourself - or that you're a failure or have let yourself or your family down?

7. Over the last two weeks, how often have you had trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television?

8. Over the last two weeks, how often have you moved or spoken so slowly that other people could have noticed? Or been so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual?

9. Over the last two weeks, how often have you had thoughts that you would be better off dead or of hurting yourself in some way?
